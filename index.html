What is Forex?

Forex, also known as foreign exchange or FX, is a global market for buying and selling currencies. It is the largest financial market in the world, with a daily trading volume of over $6 trillion. In Forex, traders buy and sell different currencies in order to profit from the fluctuations in their exchange rates.

For example, if a trader thinks that the value of the US dollar will increase against the euro, they may buy dollars and sell euros. If the value of the dollar does indeed increase, the trader can then sell their dollars for a profit. Conversely, if the value of the dollar decreases, the trader will incur a loss.

Forex trading can be done through a broker or through a marketplace like a stock exchange. It is important for traders to have a good understanding of economic and political factors that can impact exchange rates, as well as technical analysis skills to help them make informed trading decisions.

At Pips-2-Porsches, we believe that a well-rounded education is key to success in the Forex market. That's why our course covers both fundamental and technical analysis techniques. Fundamental analysis involves studying economic and political factors that can impact currency values, while technical analysis involves using charts and other tools to identify patterns and trends in the market.

Our course starts with the basics of fundamental analysis, including how to read financial statements and understand macroeconomic indicators. From there, we delve into technical analysis techniques like chart patterns, trend lines, and moving averages. By the end of the course, students will have a comprehensive understanding of both fundamental and technical analysis and how to apply them to their trading strategies.
